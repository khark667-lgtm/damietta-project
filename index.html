About the Project

Landuse / Landcover Changes is a critical tool for understanding environmental transformations. This study focuses on Damietta Governorate, analyzing how land use patterns have evolved over 23 years using satellite imagery and advanced remote sensing techniques.

Research Purpose

To monitor and quantify landcover changes in Damietta using satellite remote sensing, providing insights into urban expansion, agricultural changes, and environmental impacts.

Study area

Damietta Governorate was selected due to its strategic location at the Nile Delta, rapid urban development, and significant agricultural activities making it ideal for change detection analysis.

Importance

Understanding land cover changes is crucial for sustainable development planning, environmental protection, and informed decision-making for future urban and agricultural policies.

Why Monitor Land Cover Changes?

    Environmental Benefits

  • Track deforestation and vegetation loss
  • Monitor water body changes and coastal erosion
  • Assess agricultural land conversion
  • Identify environmental degradation patterns

    Planning Benefits

  • Support sustainable urban planning
  • Guide infrastructure development decisions
  • Inform agricultural policy and land use regulations
  • Enable evidence-based environmental management

Methodology

Our research employs advanced satellite remote sensing techniques to analyze land cover changes across three critical time periods: 2000, 2013, and 2023.

1. Data Collection

High-resolution satellite images from Landsat missions for the years 2000, 2013, and 2023. Images were selected based on cloud cover and seasonal compatibility criteria.

Sources : Landsat 7 ETM+, Landsat 8 OLI

2. Image Processing

Preprocessing includes atmospheric correction, geometric correction, and radiometric calibration. Images were co-registered and normalized to ensure accurate temporal comparison.

Tools : ENVI, ArcGIS, Google Earth Engine

3. Landcover Classification

Supervised classification using the Maximum Likelihood algorithm to determine landcover categories: urban, agricultural, water bodies, and barren lands.

Classes : Urban, Agriculture, Water, Vegetation, Bare Land

4. Changes Analysis

Post-classification comparison method applied to detect changes between time periods. Change matrices and transition maps generated to quantify landcover transformations.

Analysis : 2000-2013, 2013-2023, 2000-2023

5. Results & Validation

Accuracy assessment using ground truth data and high-resolution imagery. Statistical analysis and visualization of change patterns and trends over the 23-year period.

Accuracy : >85% overall classification accuracy

Statistics

Statistical Analysis

In this section, the classification results are statistically evaluated to assess their reliability and accuracy. Two main indicators are used: Overall Accuracy (OA) and the Kappa Coefficient (κ).

Overall Accuracy (OA) : represents the percentage of correctly classified pixels compared to the total reference data, providing a straightforward measure of performance.

Kappa Coefficient (κ) : is a more robust statistical index that measures the degree of agreement between the classification results and the reference data, while correcting for the agreement that might occur by chance. Values of κ range between 0 and 1, where values above 0.8 generally indicate strong agreement, values between 0.6 and 0.8 moderate agreement, and values below 0.6 reflect weaker classification reliability.

For each study year, the OA and κ values are reported separately to highlight the classification quality of 2000, 2013, and 2023.

    In addition, change detection statistics are presented for the three temporal intervals :

  • 2000–2013 (baseline to first change period)
  • 2013–2023 (second change period)
  • 2000–2023 (overall long-term change)

These statistics are expressed in terms of percentage changes for each land cover class, supported by charts and tabular results. Together, they provide a clear picture of the classification reliability and the dynamics of land cover transformations over time.

Key Findings

    Major Trends

  • Rapid urbanization with 87% increase in built-up areas
  • Agricultural land conversion to urban use
  • Coastal modifications affecting water boundaries
  • Infrastructure development along major corridors

    Environmental Impact

  • Loss of prime agricultural land to development
  • Changes in local hydrology and drainage patterns
  • Increased impervious surface coverage
  • Potential impacts on local ecosystem services
