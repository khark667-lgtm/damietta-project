Land Cover Change Detection in Damietta

(2000–2013–2023)

Using Remote Sensing to Monitor Environmental Changes Across Time

Maps

About the Project

Land cover change detection is a critical tool for understanding environmentaltransformations. This study focuses on Damietta Governorate, analyzing how land use patterns have evolved over 23 years using satellite imagery and advanced remote sensing techniques.

Research Purpose

To monitor and quantify land cover changes in Damietta using satellite remote sensing, providing insights into urban expansion, agricultural changes, and environmental impacts.

Area of Study

Damietta Governorate was selected due to its strategic location at the Nile Delta, rapid urban development, and significant agricultural activities making it ideal for change detection analysis.

Importance

Understanding land cover changes is crucial for sustainable development planning, environmental protection, and informed decision-making for future urban and agricultural policies.

Why Monitor Land Cover Changes?

    Environmental Benefits

  • Track deforestation and vegetation loss
  • Monitor water body changes and coastal erosion
  • Assess agricultural land conversion
  • Identify environmental degradation patterns

    Planning Benefits

  • Support sustainable urban planning
  • Guide infrastructure development decisions
  • Inform agricultural policy and land use regulations
  • Enable evidence-based environmental management
